Tool stack

Best AI Tools for Solo Business Owners

A solo owner does not need every AI tool. A useful starter stack covers writing, customer replies, meeting notes, simple automation, and reporting.

Start with jobs, not tool names

Choose tools by the job they handle. If a tool does not save time on a weekly task, it may be interesting but not necessary.

A practical starter stack

  • General AI assistant for drafting and summarizing.
  • Meeting notes tool for calls and action items.
  • Email assistant for replies and follow-ups.
  • Automation tool for moving data between apps.
  • Spreadsheet or dashboard tool for weekly reporting.

What to avoid

Avoid paying for five overlapping writing tools. Avoid tools that need too much setup before they create value. Avoid uploading sensitive customer information unless you understand privacy and data settings.

Evaluation checklist

  1. Does it save at least one hour per week?
  2. Does it fit your current workflow?
  3. Can you explain what it does in one sentence?
  4. Can you cancel easily if it does not work?
